01 Executive Summary & Process Overview

The Running Account Bill (RA Bill) is the cornerstone financial instrument in construction project management. It represents a periodic claim submitted by a contractor against the value of work executed under a Work Order (WO), adjusted for mobilisation advances, material supplies, retention holdbacks, and contractual escalation/de-escalation clauses. For Casagrand — one of India's leading real estate developers operating across 25+ cities — the RA Bill process governs the disbursement of hundreds of crores annually to civil, MEP, interior, and specialty contractors.

This document establishes the enterprise-grade standard operating procedure (SOP) for the entire RA Bill lifecycle — from contractor onboarding through final bill settlement — covering services, materials, project execution monitoring, compliance, financial adjustments, and executive reporting. The process is designed to be SAP-enabled, with the reference backend (S/4HANA Hana PEOL) providing the architectural blueprint for table structures, document flows, and integration points.

2.2 Work Order (WO) Placement — SAP Purchase Order

A Work Order in Casagrand's context is an SAP Purchase Order (PO) of a specific document type mapped to the project. The WO contains the complete Bill of Quantities (BOQ), milestone schedule, commercial terms, retention clauses, escalation formula, and compliance requirements. Every RA Bill is processed against a specific WO number — the WO is the parent reference document throughout the payment lifecycle.

🔗 SAP Reference: The backend supports document type NB (Standard PO, BSTYP=B) and FO (Framework/Blanket PO, BSTYP=B) per T161 configuration. For Casagrand, recommend custom document types: ZWO (Work Order — Lumpsum), ZWR (Work Order — Rate Contract), ZWM (Work Order — Material Supply).

⚠️ Critical Rule — WO as Ceiling

The cumulative value of all RA Bills against a Work Order shall never exceed the total WO value (including approved variations/change orders). Any bill that would breach the WO ceiling must trigger a Change Order (CO) approval before processing. The SAP system must enforce this through PO tolerance checks.

3.4 RA Bill — Cumulative vs Current Period

A critical concept in RA Bill processing is the cumulative method. Each RA Bill shows the total work done from inception to date, and the "current bill amount" is the difference between the current cumulative and the previous cumulative. This prevents double-counting and ensures the payment trail is always reconcilable.

4.2 Service Entry Sheet (SES) — SAP Process

1

Create SES (ML81N)

The Quantity Surveyor (QS) creates a Service Entry Sheet against the Work Order PO in SAP transaction ML81N. Each SES line maps to a BOQ item with measured quantity. The SES can have multiple service lines covering different BOQ items within the same bill period.

2

Technical Acceptance

The SES triggers a workflow notification to the Project Manager (PM) for technical review. The PM verifies: (a) quantities match the signed Measurement Book, (b) quality parameters are met (cube test results, inspection reports), (c) no defective work is being claimed.

3

Release SES

After PM certification, the SES is released (accepted). Released SES status = "Accepted" in SAP. This is equivalent to Goods Receipt for services and creates the accrual posting in FI.

4

Invoice Verification (MIRO)

The contractor's RA Bill invoice is posted in MIRO with reference to the released SES. The system auto-populates quantities and values from the SES. Commercial adjustments (escalation, retention, advance recovery) are applied as pricing conditions.

✅ Three-Way Match for Services

Work Order (PO) ↔ Service Entry Sheet (SES) ↔ Invoice (MIRO). All three must agree on quantities and values within tolerance limits. Any variance beyond ±2% triggers automatic hold for QS review.

05 Materials — RA Bill for Material Supply

While the majority of RA Bills are for services (labour + materials combined), certain contracts are structured as material-only supply — where the contractor supplies specific building materials (ready-mix concrete, steel reinforcement, façade panels, tiles, sanitary ware) and Casagrand's own workforce or another contractor installs them. Additionally, some service contracts have a material component where Casagrand free-issues materials to the contractor and recovers the cost from RA Bills.

5.1 Material Supply — Two Scenarios

ScenarioDescriptionSAP ProcessRA Bill Impact
A: Contractor Supplies Material Contractor procures and delivers material to site as part of WO scope. Material cost is embedded in BOQ rates. GRN via MIGO → Invoice via MIRO → Payment via F110. Standard 3-way match: PO ↔ GRN ↔ Invoice RA Bill includes material value in gross work value. No separate material recovery.
B: Casagrand Free-Issues Material Casagrand procures materials directly (cement, steel, bricks) and issues to contractor. Contractor's WO covers labour-only or reduced-rate items. Casagrand PO to material supplier → GRN at site → Issue to contractor via 261 movement type (consumption posting). Recovery from contractor's RA Bill. Material cost recovered from contractor's RA Bill as "Material Advance Recovery" (Component G). Pro-rata or at actual consumption.

🚨 Material Wastage Rule

If material wastage exceeds the permissible limit (typically 2-5% depending on material type as per IS codes), the excess wastage value is deducted as penalty from the contractor's RA Bill. The permissible wastage limits must be defined in the Work Order terms. Example: Cement wastage > 3% → contractor bears excess cost at ₹ 420/bag (current market rate).

6.3 Physical Progress Tracking — % Completion Method

The cornerstone metric for Casagrand's project monitoring is the relationship between Physical % Completion and Financial % Disbursement. This dual-axis tracking prevents over-billing and ensures the contractor's payment is proportional to actual work delivered. The calculation methodology is:

Physical Completion %
Physical % = (Σ Measured_Qty × Weightage_Factor) / (Σ BOQ_Qty × Weightage_Factor) × 100

Financial Disbursement %
Financial % = (Cumulative_Net_Payments) / (Total_WO_Value) × 100

Health Ratio
Health Ratio = Physical % / Financial %
→ Ratio > 1.0 = Under-billed (healthy) | Ratio < 1.0 = Over-billed (risk) | Ratio = 1.0 = On track

08 Adjustment of Advances

Advances are a critical financial mechanism in construction contracting. They provide the contractor with working capital to mobilise resources, procure materials, and set up site infrastructure. However, advances represent a financial risk to Casagrand — every rupee advanced must be systematically recovered through RA Bills and secured through bank guarantees. This section defines the types of advances, their limits, recovery mechanisms, and SAP posting methodology.

8.2 Advance Recovery Calculation

Mobilisation Advance Recovery — Per RA Bill
Recovery_Amount = Gross_RA_Bill_Value × Recovery_Rate%

Where Recovery Rate
Recovery_Rate% = (Advance_Amount / WO_Value) × (100% / (100% − Advance%))

Example — ₹10 Cr WO, 10% Advance (₹1 Cr)
Recovery per RA Bill = Gross Value × 11.11% (so that advance is fully recovered by ~90% completion)
The 11.11% rate ensures the ₹1 Cr advance is fully recovered by the time 90% of the work (₹9 Cr) is billed. Remaining 10% WO value (₹1 Cr) has no recovery deduction.

⚠️ Advance Recovery — Safeguards

  • If cumulative recovery exceeds advance amount (rounding), cap recovery at advance balance — never over-recover.
  • If contractor is terminated mid-contract, unrecovered advance is claimed against the bank guarantee.
  • Bank guarantee must remain valid until advance is fully recovered + 3 months buffer.
  • If BG expires before full recovery, all payments halted until BG is renewed.

10 Escalations & De-Escalations

Construction contracts spanning 12+ months are subject to price fluctuations in key input materials (cement, steel, fuel, labour wages). To share this risk fairly between Casagrand and the contractor, Work Orders include a Price Variation Clause (PVC) — also called an Escalation/De-Escalation clause. This clause adjusts the RA Bill value based on movement in published price indices.

10.1 Escalation Formula — CPWD / IRC Standard

Price Adjustment Factor (PAF)
PAF = 0.85 × [(W₁ × L₁/L₀) + (W₂ × C₁/C₀) + (W₃ × S₁/S₀) + (W₄ × F₁/F₀) + (W₅ × B₁/B₀)] + 0.15

Where:
W₁ to W₅ = Weightage factors for Labour, Cement, Steel, Fuel, Bitumen (sum = 1.0)
L₁, C₁, S₁, F₁, B₁ = Current period index values
L₀, C₀, S₀, F₀, B₀ = Base period index values (at WO award date)
0.15 = Fixed component (15% of WO value is non-escalable — overheads, profit)

PAF Calculation
PAF = 0.85 × 1.0700 + 0.15 = 0.9095 + 0.15 = 1.0595

Escalation %
Escalation = (PAF − 1) × 100 = 5.95%

If Current RA Bill Gross = ₹ 2,30,00,000
Escalation Amount = ₹ 2,30,00,000 × 5.95% = ₹ 13,68,500

📊 De-Escalation — Symmetrical Application

If indices fall below the base period (PAF < 1.0), the same formula applies symmetrically — resulting in a negative adjustment (de-escalation) that reduces the RA Bill value. Casagrand's policy mandates that escalation/de-escalation applies only when the PAF deviates by more than ±2% from 1.0 (dead band). Within the ±2% band, no adjustment is made.

10.4 Escalation Governance Rules

#RuleDescription
1Cap on EscalationMaximum escalation payable per RA Bill period shall not exceed 10% of the base BOQ rate. Any excess escalation requires VP-Projects approval.
2Index VerificationIndices must be verified from the official published source (Labour Bureau / DPIIT website). Contractor's claims based on market surveys are not accepted.
3Retrospective ApplicationIf the index for a billing period is not yet published, the last published index is used provisionally. Adjustment is made in the next RA Bill when actual index is available.
4Fixed-Price ContractsFor contracts ≤ 12 months duration, escalation clause is not applicable. Rates are firm and fixed.
5Escalation on Delayed WorkIf the contractor is behind schedule (as per approved bar chart), escalation is calculated on indices applicable to the originally scheduled period, not the actual (delayed) period. The contractor bears the price risk for their own delay.
